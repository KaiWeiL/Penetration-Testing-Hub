color: rgb(6, 69, 173); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Ronald Wayne</a>&nbsp;in April 1976 to develop and sell Wozniak's&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_I" title="Apple I" style="text-decoration: none; color: rgb(6, 69, 173); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Apple I</a>&nbsp;personal computer, though Wayne sold his share back to Jobs and Wozniak within 12 days. It was incorporated as&nbsp;<b>Apple Computer, Inc.</b>, in January 1977, and sales of its computers, including the&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_II" title="Apple II" style="text-decoration: none; color: rgb(6, 69, 173); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Apple II</a>, grew quickly.</p><p style="margin: 0.5em 0px; color: rgb(32, 33, 34); font-size: 14px; font-weight: 400;">Jobs and Wozniak hired a staff of computer designers and had a production line starting in Jobs' garage. Apple&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering" style="text-decoration: none; color: rgb(6, 69, 173); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">went public</a>&nbsp;in 1980 to instant financial success. Over the next few years, Apple shipped new computers featuring innovative&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graphical_user_interface" title="Graphical user interface" style="text-decoration: none; color: rgb(6, 69, 173); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">graphical user interfaces</a>, such as the&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macintosh_128K" title="Macintosh 128K" style="text-decoration: none; color: rgb(6, 69, 173); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">original Macintosh</a>&nbsp;in 1984, and Apple's marketing advertisements for its products received widespread critical acclaim. However, the high price of its products and limited application library caused problems, as did power struggles between executives.
